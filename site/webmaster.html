WHAT'S THIS GUY'S DEAL...

I'm lowkenuinely a nobody — or a somebody, if you want me to be. I've been tinkering with computers since I was 6 years old, so you could call me a kind of nerd. My earliest memory was playing random car-related browser games on the computers in my dad’s office. I wish I could try those games again, but they’re kind of lost to time now…

Nevertheless, my online presence is, arguably, small. I had a Discord account at the ripe age of 8 — but hey, who didn’t? I did a few things here and there, somehow became a moderator in a server, and was a pretty active editor on a game’s wiki page. Standard pre-teen digital footprint

It wasn’t until late in the pandemic that I gained full awareness — what I like to call the release of version 2 of myself into the public. It’s strange, you know? From what I remember, original me would’ve hated V2 me — mindsets and all. But that’s life. What are humans without drastic change? You can’t be the same person your whole life, can you?

I think I've been yammering a little bit too long and missed the whole point of this section, what really is my deal?

In the grand scheme of the universe, I see myself as an irrelevant speck of dust. Not worthy of being remembered — not worthy of being mentioned

And I hate that

I hate that I have nothing to my name. No talent I’ve mastered. No magnum opus worthy of pointing at and saying, that was mine. I hate that if I died tomorrow, there would be nothing substantial left behind as proof that I was ever here

So in the wake I leave behind, I try to make an impression — to etch my persona into people’s minds. Almost everything I do is performative to some degree

Except maybe sleep
Maybe music
Maybe games

Okay — there are a few exceptions

That’s why I create. That’s why this website exists. That’s why there are a hundred unfinished story drafts sitting somewhere on my computer. You might think that’s a lame existence — “This guy only exists to be acknowledged”

And honestly? You wouldn’t be entirely wrong.

I don’t know if the craving for acknowledgment comes from some deep-rooted psychological fracture that formed when I was six or seven — but it’s there. And pretending otherwise would be dishonest

So my deal?

I exist — and I want people to know that I exist

It’s ironic. I’m an introvert by nature, yet I crave recognition. Another contradiction to add to the list of questions I’ll ask God when I’m gone
